### The Brutal Truth About How Buyers Actually Read Your Emails

Your prospects aren't reading your emails word for word. Research shows that executives spend approximately three seconds scanning a cold email before deciding whether to engage or hit delete. For other prospects, you might get a generous nine seconds. That's your entire window of opportunity.

### Why Inboxes Have Become Battlegrounds

According to Martal's 2025 analysis, cold email open rates dropped from 36% in 2023 to just 27.7% in 2024. And of those who do open, Belkins' 2024 research found that reply rates dropped to just 5.8%, down from 6.8% the year before. AI has made it incredibly easy to send thousands of personalized-looking emails with minimal effort. The result is that decision-makers now receive 10 or more irrelevant cold emails every single week.

### The Scanning Reality That Changes Everything

When prospects open your email, they're scanning for context in seconds — pattern recognition mode, not comprehension mode. They're looking for visual cues, assessing how much mental energy it will take to process, deciding if it's relevant right now, and checking if there's a clear action. If your email requires careful reading, scrolling on a phone, or looks like a dense block of text, they'll delete it.

### The Mental Load Problem Nobody Talks About

Every email adds to their mental load. When your email is long, complicated, or requires significant mental effort to understand, it gets deleted immediately — not because they're rude, but because they literally don't have the mental capacity to process one more complex thing.

### Why Your Perfect Subject Line Is Failing You (The Preview Text Trap)

Sellers obsess over the subject line while ignoring the first ten words that appear below it in the inbox preview. When prospects scan, they see two things together: your subject line and your preview text. If your subject line is intriguing but your preview text is generic fluff ("I hope this email finds you well"), you've broken the promise.

### The Mobile Reality You're Ignoring

More than half of emails are now first opened on mobile devices. Can your entire email be read without scrolling on a phone? If not, you're losing people.

### The Grade Level Mistake That's Costing You Deals

Writing at a fifth-grade level has nothing to do with intelligence. It has everything to do with scannability. Short sentences are easier to scan. Simple words require less cognitive processing. White space creates visual breaks. (Leslie shoots for grade five in all her emails.)

### What Actually Works: The Shift You Need to Make

Stop writing emails that need to be read. Start writing emails that can be scanned. Ruthlessly cut anything that doesn't serve the core message. Use short sentences. Leave white space. Make your call to action crystal clear. And ask before you hit send: "Have I earned the right to ask for this person's attention?"

### My "Too Damn Long" Wake-Up Call

Leslie shares a story about selling to the energy sector and a crotchety former military power plant manager named Ashley — one of her best clients. She'd send long, value-packed follow-up emails crammed with every link. One day Ashley called and said: "Leslie, I got to tell you, your emails are too damn long." The lesson: More isn't better. Value-packed doesn't mean valuable if nobody can process it. Even your best clients will ignore you if you make it too hard to engage.

### Making Your Emails Unignorable — The 1-10-100 Rule

- **1** clear call to action.
- **10** optimized words in your preview text, including your subject line.
- **100** words maximum for your email body.

This framework forces you to prioritize scannability, mobile optimization, and respect for your prospect's attention. The 10 is optimizing the first ten words (including subject line) to give prospects a reason to open and read more. The 100 is keeping it under 100 words so the entire email can be read without scrolling on a phone. Test it — send it to yourself and open it on your phone.

According to Belkins' analysis of 16.5 million cold emails, most sellers are getting ignored most of the time, with reply rates hovering around 5.8% in 2024. The ones who aren't being ignored have figured out that scanning behavior isn't a problem to overcome. It's reality to design for.

### Additional tactical guidance (from FAQs)

- **Email length:** under 100 words total.
- **Formatting:** Avoid bold/heavy formatting — when people scan, bolded words become the only thing they read, so they miss your full message. Excessive formatting also triggers mental spam filters.
- **Sequence length:** Minimum of eight touch points. Most sellers stop after 2-3 emails; even a mediocre eighth email gets responses because most competition has given up.
- **CTAs:** Give only one. Multiple options divide attention and the probability of either action plummets.
- **Sound human, not like AI:** Use your actual voice and contractions. AI is good at surfacing insights, but never ship a fully AI-written email — it has the staccato writing, "no fluff" phrases, em dashes, and "game changers."
- **Escalating CTAs:** Start sequences with "CTA lights" ("worth a chat?"), then share a link/asset, then directly ask for time at the end — paralleling the earn-the-right philosophy of building trust before bigger asks.
