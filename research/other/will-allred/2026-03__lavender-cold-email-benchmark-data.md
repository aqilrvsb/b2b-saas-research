## Lavender cold-email benchmark learnings (2026)
Source: Lavender blog, authored by Will Allred (article index, lavender.ai/blog)

- **Emailing Operations leaders** — When sellers write "A-grade" emails to operations personas, the
  reply rate climbs to **5.4%**, a **+58% lift** over average. *Takeaway: ops leaders DO reply to cold
  email; they just need to instantly understand the specific reason they should care.*
  (https://www.lavender.ai/blog/benchmark-learnings-emailing-operations — Mar 30, 2026)
- **Emailing Finance leaders** — Only **6.1%** of emails to finance earn an "A" grade (the lowest of any
  persona), yet A-grade emails to finance see a **+79% lift** in reply rate (the *highest* lift of any
  persona). *Takeaway: finance is the hardest persona to write well for, but the payoff for getting it
  right is the largest.*
  (https://www.lavender.ai/blog/benchmark-learnings-emailing-finance — Mar 24, 2026)
- **The Cold Email Benchmark Report** — Interactive report benchmarking your cold email performance
  against the billions of emails Lavender has analyzed across ~50k inboxes.

## The Sales Email Cadence framework (Lavender)
Source: https://www.lavender.ai/blog/sales-email-cadence — "Your cadence should pull your prospect in, not push them away."
Opening move of a good cold email:
1. **Make an observation** — one sentence that shows you know your reader (use your personalization research).
2. **Tie that observation to a problem** they likely have.

## "Getting Past the 3-Second Filter" (Will Allred)
Source: Close.com roundup quoting Will Allred's LinkedIn post (100k-view poll)
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_so-my-poll-on-friday-blew-up-100k-views-activity-6876154411899531264-BPVd/

Most cold emails are deleted in ~3 seconds. Will's four keys to keep a prospect reading:
1. **Formatting** — short, skimmable, mobile-friendly (most emails are read on a phone).
2. **Clarity** — one clear idea, no jargon.
3. **Brevity** — short enough to read in the preview pane.
4. **Written-for-them** — it must be obvious the email was written for this person, not blasted.

### Related Lavender / Allred principle frequently cited by other experts
"Personalization drives 50–250% more replies — *but only if the personalization is tied to the
problem.*" Personalization for its own sake (e.g. "love your hiking photos") does not lift replies;
personalization connected to a relevant problem does.
