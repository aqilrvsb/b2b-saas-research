A Q&A between Vaibhav Namburi and Nick Abraham (Founder, Leadbird) on cold emailing, tools, target metrics, and scaling a performance-based lead gen agency.

## Q1: Why Do People Pay You?

Nick: "Yes. So on Scrubby, it's simply because we're the only solution in a market that can actually validate the catch-alls. What makes us stand out is that we've built a pretty full tech stack and use Smartlead as well."

"But we work on performance, so our clients pay us per every lead we generate. So it's kind of like eating our own dog food and making sure that we have to generate results for clients and not just take on retainers like a lot of the other competitors in the space."

## Q2: Why Did You Opt For a Performance-Based Model?

Nick: "Yeah. So I opted for it because our cost of goods is so low because we own the entire tech sector."

"The reason why we're able to get good results is because we'll choose and be selective about the clients we bring on. And if they have unique value props or just like a unique offer with a good target, we're pretty good when it comes to getting results."

## Q3: Top Three Key Automations an Agency Should Try When Scaling?

Nick: "Yeah. So I would say onboarding, you need to run a lot of automation through there. Collecting all that information and putting it in the right places."

"The other ones I would say that are easy are reporting. Making sure that the weekly reports go out. It's completely hands-off, automated, very easy to do."

"Third one, which is my personal favorite, is we typically do a checking call every month with our clients." (Phone calls help build a less-formal relationship with the client.)

## Q4: 10 Million Cold Emails — Data Insights

Nick: "Yeah. So honestly, I personally think I've sent more than 10 million. I just kind of put 10 million as a safe line number so I am not overboard."

"But you know what's been really cool the last six months — I used to not really collect a lot of data, and so we never really looked at a lot of different things. But once we started collecting data, it's so much easier to know how to get results specifically since we work on performance."

He tracks reply rates, positive reply rates, not-interested reply rates, unsubscribe rates, and how many people are added into the info sequence.

## Q5: Good Metrics to Aim For (Open Rate, Positive Reply Rate, Bounce Rate)?

Nick: "Yeah. So it's super hard to say, because every product is different. So whenever clients come to us, I try to stay away from giving a baseline metric that I try to hit because it really is completely different for each client."

Rather than a baseline, he treats each client differently based on their market, setting an improvement target month after month, keeping the bounce rate as low as possible and reply rates higher.

## Q6: Handling Offers/Frameworks That Never Work?

Nick: "Like what I said before, you would see the worst offer, and you just send enough cold emails, you're going to get some leads that come through the pipeline, but before I push a client to reposition or restructure their offer..."

"What I try to do is just hit the bare bones... cleaning it up and throwing more social proof on it, making sure that their LinkedIn profile is coming up, making sure that if they have a G2 review and it's a bad review, trying to figure out ways to remediate that — just cleaning up the things outside of the offer that will help them have more of a brand presence."

"If your campaign still isn't working, what I try to do is find a whole bunch of industries that they have case studies in and then present those case studies and target those industries."

"On the copywriting framework, ideally your first email is a super direct response. But if that's not getting results because the offer is generic or saturated, make the email super conversational and just focus on getting a reply, then put a lot more work on the inbox management to convert those replies into leads."

## Q7: Recommended Tools for Cold Emailing + Management?

Nick: "We've built our own email platform... but there are situations where the recipient may have a bad event, and it goes into your inbox spam folder... the platform is not going to capture that... people that email you outside of the thread — there are so many scenarios that some master inboxes don't capture at all."

"When we were using our email tool, everything looked good, and then we logged into the individual inboxes, and we were like, oh wow, I think we might have lost over $40,000 to $50,000 opportunities from people requesting info that we just never got back. So I prefer to keep them separate just to be extra safe."

"If you have under 100 inboxes, you could use Google [and] forward everything into there. If you have over 100 inboxes, then you need something like a shared inbox like Trust [what we use and recommend]. And for emails I think we're using Smartlead."

## Q8: Three Changes in the Past Three Months With Highest Revenue Impact?

1. Hiring skilled people and paying them accordingly: "We have a phenomenal copywriter and a phenomenal inbox manager. That has been one thing that's kind of taken me out of a lot of the fulfillment stuff. That has been a huge driver."
2. Collecting social proof: "Three months ago, we had zero testimonials on our site. Right now, we have 12 on G2, five out of five stars. All we're doing right now is just trying to build as much social proof as possible."
3. Select partners: "We found some select partners that want to white label our service."

## Q9: How Do You Handle Blacklisted Mailboxes?

Nick: "Yeah. So we'll try to just burn it and move on, for the most part. But I'm not entirely sure if that's the greatest idea, to be honest. So we're also looking at different alternatives."

## Q10: Current Configuration?

Nick: "We've adjusted it a little bit, and we're still kind of playing with it... I try to do a 1-to-1 match with our warm to old emails and keep the send volume per inbox under 60 per day. So we're doing 30 warm and 30 cold." (Testing this with all clients that month.)

## Q11: Thoughts on Open Rates?

Nick: "From a cold email perspective, you don't really need to worry too much about open rates. I think the biggest thing is inbox placement... I think that will always be the golden standard for testing." He emphasizes inbox placement over open rates.
